## 1. What to Look For in a Supplier

### Must-Haves ✅

| Requirement | Why It Matters |
|-------------|----------------|
| **Certificate of Analysis (CoA)** | Proves purity & identity. Must be batch-specific, not generic |
| **98%+ Purity** | Lower purity = more side effects, less effectiveness |
| **Third-Party Testing** | Independent lab testing = unbiased verification |
| **Batch Numbers** | Every batch should have its own CoA |
| **Molecular Weight Verification** | Confirms correct peptide sequence |
| **HPLC & Mass Spec Data** | Shows actual purity, not just label claims |

### Red Flags 🚩

| Warning Sign | What It Means |
|--------------|---------------|
| No CoA available | Can't verify what you're selling |
| Generic/shared CoA | They reuse documents across batches |
| Refuses to test | Likely knows purity is low |
| No batch numbers | No traceability |
| Prices too good | Usually means low purity |
| No response to questions | Walk away |

### What a Real CoA Should Include:
- Peptide name & amino acid sequence
- **Purity percentage** (98%+ target)
- Molecular weight (matches expected)
- HPLC chromatogram
- Mass spectrometry data
- Testing lab name
- Batch number & date
- Analyst signature

## 3. How to Vet a New Supplier

### Step 1: Request Samples
- Order 1-2g of 2-3 different peptides
- Test BPC-157, CJC-1295 (common, well-documented)

### Step 2: Verify the CoA
- Check batch number matches
- Verify testing lab is independent
- Compare HPLC peaks to known-good samples

### Step 3: Self-Test (Optional but Smart)
- Use third-party testing lab ($50-100 per peptide)
- **Recommended labs:** 
  - EZBiologics
  -袁 Codetober
  - SGS (expensive but thorough)

### Step 4: Start Small
- First order: 10-50g
- Test quality before committing to larger orders
